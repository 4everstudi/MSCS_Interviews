# Interview Questions

•	On the Internet you can find many different features of Microservices described. Do you agree or disagree with the following statements:
    o	Individual microservices should not have more than 10-100 LOCs.
    o	Microservices should be developed by a team that can be fed by 1 or 2 pizzas.
    o	Microservice architecture should avoid synchronous communication.
    o	Microservices should be maintained by the team who developed them.
    o	Microservices should manage their own databases.
    o	Individual microservices should match DDD bounded contexts.
    o	Microservices should be dynamically discovered.
    o	Microservices should be small enough to be re-written within one sprint.
•	What is your definition of microservices? Which additional features can you describe (based on your experience)?
•	Has the transition from a monolithic system to a collection of microservices been completed in your organization? If so, how were those legacy systems converted to microservices?
•	What are/were the three most important challenges on your way to mi- croservices (so far)?
•	Where are microservices hosted in your organization? (public cloud ser- vices/private cloud/combined)?
•	How are success and ROI measured after the introduction of individual microservices? Which KPIs are most important for you?
•	How does the lifecycle of a microservice looks like in your organization? (Starting with requirements up to the introduction)
    o	What is your release strategy? (independent deployments/release trains)
    o	How many environments (dev/test/staging/production etc.) are used for the development?
•	How do you manage microservice configurations in your organization? Which tools are used? (Puppet, Ansible etc.)
•	How are your microservices coupled between each other?
•	What architectural microservice patterns are essential for your applicati- on(s)? (application/application infrastructure/infrastructure patterns)
•	How is the data integrity ensured by your microservice application(s)?
